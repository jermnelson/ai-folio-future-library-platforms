Token Affirmation on RDF Triples

Single-token affirmation is neither practical nor desirable; however, affirming tokens at the statement level presents an opportunity for cultural heritage institutions. The core assertion of RDF graphs is the triple statement composed of subject, predicate, and object. Vectors of these statements can be generated from various models, and by publishing these vectors, institutions implicitly affirm that the source statements reflect their authority.
