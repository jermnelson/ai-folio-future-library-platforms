AI Impacts All Levels of the Library

Large Language Models (LLMs) are the first wide-spread generalist artificial intelligence. A recent report by Antropic's economists, tries to measure the impacts of AI on labor markets. They first estimate an occupation's number of tasks that could theoretical be done by AI followed by the observed AI use in meeting those tasks.

Libraries (and higher education) have a higher observed exposure to AI so that of the tasks AI theoretically could speed-up or take over is large, Antropic observed usage of AI on those tasks in libraries as also being higher.

Patrons, Librarians, and Library Staff likely use generative AI in a variety ways. For example:

  • Patrons: Ask reference librarians for assistance in finding sources mentioned in chat sessions with LLMs
  • Administrators: LLMs assist in analysis and writing reports to library stakeholders
  • Catalogers: Use LLMs to summarize electronic text, assist in Subject Headings Assignment
  • Circulation: LLMs assist in patron communication via email or chat
Open Question: How should (or can) our Library Systems, specifically FOLIO, support everyone's use of AI?
