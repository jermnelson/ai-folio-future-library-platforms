External & Internal AI Agents

AI agents are software systems that use AI to pursue goals and complete tasks on behalf of users. They show reasoning, planning, and memory and have a level of autonomy to make decisions, learn, and adapt.

What is an AI agent? From Google Cloud

Dimension External AI Agents Internal AI Agents
Ownership Vendor/third party/open source Library/consortium
Primary goal General productivity / coding / research Library-specific services (discovery, metadata, reference, operations)
Data use Pulls from public web + library resources Prioritizes licensed/local collections + policies
Control & policy Limited control High control (guardrails, logging, retention)
Risk profileHigher (privacy, licensing, provenance) Lower if governed well, but requires staffing

External AI Agents

General AI agents that, while not specific to libraries, use existing libraries services and resources to complete tasks.

  • Coding/Software development agents - (Claude Code, Codex) assist staff in integrating APIs and automating workflows
  • Workflow agents - (Openclaw) Integrate and automate disparate workflows. Not for the weak of heart!
  • Research Agents - (Perplexity, NotebookLM) research

Internal AI Agents

The development and deployment of library specific AI has tended to be on the discovery and metadata, reference chatbot as enhancements to existing commercial or open-source platforms.

Other areas with ongoing research efforts

  • Collections & acquisitions analyze usage/costs, summarizing contracts
  • Digital scholarship audio/video transcription, OCR corrections
  • Operations policy assistance, news and event communication
